Basic Anatomy of a Website

Header

The header is the top section of a website, where you’ll usually find the logo and the site’s navigation menu...

Pre-Header

The pre-header is the thin strip that sits above the main header. It’s often used to display helpful information...

Favicon

A favicon is the small icon that appears in a browser tab next to your website title...

Navigation (Main Menu)

The navigation is the set of links at the top of a website that helps visitors find what they’re looking for...

Breadcrumbs

Breadcrumbs are a type of navigation that shows visitors their location within a website’s hierarchy...

Homepage

The homepage — also called the front page — is the starting point of most websites...

Sidebar

A sidebar is the narrow vertical column that appears alongside your main website content...

Sub-Footer

The sub-footer is the section that appears at the bottom of a webpage—just above the main footer...

  • Quick Contact Information
  • Supplementary Navigation Links
  • Social Media Icons
  • Additional Resources

Footer

The footer is the final, definitive section at the very bottom of a webpage. It typically contains essential legal and informational details...

  • Copyright
  • Policy Links
  • Site Map
